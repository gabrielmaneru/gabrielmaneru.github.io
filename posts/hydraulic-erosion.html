Hydraulic Erosion AI

Instructor: Iker Silvano

Languages: C++

Tools: OpenGL, GLFW

Research: Hydraulic Erosion - Paper

Release: GitHub

Overview:

The goal of this course was to understand a wide range of concepts and practical algorithms that are commonly used to solve game AI problems. Topics covered included AI architecture (state-machines, goal-based systems, smart terrain, message passing), pathfinding, agent awareness, agent cooperation, terrain analysis and planning.

As a final project I choose to research about possible usages of AI in procedural world generation. I decided to write my research about Hydraulic Erosion simulating natural processes in order to reproduce as fair as possible the natural development of a terrain. The demo simulates the erosion where particles try to mimic water flowing down the slope redistributing the sediment. The implementation is highly adaptable to different types of terrains and the result can be adjusted at runtime through a wide range of parameters.
